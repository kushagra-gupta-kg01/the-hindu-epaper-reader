SC upholds SIR, says it is EC’s constitutional duty

By Legal Correspondent
NEW DELHI, MAY 28

The Supreme Court of India on Thursday upheld the constitutional validity of the Single-ID Registration (SIR) system for the national voter registry, ruling that it is the Election Commission's (EC) core constitutional duty to ensure an authenticated and duplicate-free list of eligible voters.

A three-judge Bench, led by Chief Justice of India, dismissed a batch of petitions challenging the statutory amendments that enabled the linkage of voter identity records with central database indices.

"The purity of the electoral roll is the foundation of free and fair elections. The Election Commission holds a constitutional mandate under Article 324 to ensure database sanitisation is carried out transparently and safely."

The petitioners had argued that the integration of databases could lead to massive disenfranchisement of vulnerable communities and raised critical concerns regarding data privacy. However, the court accepted the government's submissions that robust security protocols are in place to safeguard database keys.

Protests erupted outside the Election Commission headquarters shortly after the verdict was announced, with opposition leaders claiming the ruling compromises privacy standards. The ruling is expected to accelerate database linkages ahead of next year's civic body elections.
